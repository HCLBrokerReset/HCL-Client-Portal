# Hola Herron 🇪🇸 — Spanish for Barry & Sarah

A simple, friendly app for learning to **speak** Spanish together on the couch.
Every phrase follows a **teach → copy → speak** rhythm: the app says it in a
Spain (Castilian) accent, you repeat it, then you say it yourself and the app
listens and checks you. It starts with genuinely useful holiday Spanish and
builds all the way up to GCSE-level grammar (past, future and conditional tenses).

It's a **web app** — there's nothing to download from the App Store. You open a
link once, add it to your home screen, and it behaves exactly like a normal app
icon: full screen, works offline, and remembers your progress.

---

## 📱 Getting it onto your iPhones (one-time setup)

**Step 1 — Publish the app (do this once):**
1. On GitHub, go to this repo → **Settings** → **Pages**.
2. Under **Build and deployment → Source**, choose **GitHub Actions**.
3. Merge this branch into `main` (or push to `main`). The included workflow
   then publishes the app automatically and gives you a link like
   `https://<your-username>.github.io/HCL-Client-Portal/`.

**Step 2 — Add it to each phone:**
1. Open that link in **Safari** (it must be Safari, not Chrome, for the mic and
   home-screen features to work properly on iPhone).
2. Tap the **Share** button (the square with the up-arrow).
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen** → **Add**.
4. You'll get a "Hola Herron" icon. Open it from there — it runs full screen.

Do this on both your phone and Sarah's. Each phone keeps its own progress.

> The very first time you tap the 🎤 microphone, iPhone will ask permission to
> use the mic — tap **Allow**.

## 🛋️ How to use it each night

1. Open the app and tap your name (**Barry** or **Sarah**).
2. Tap **▶︎ Tonight's session**. It mixes a few review phrases (the ones you
   found tricky before) with the next new lesson — about 20–30 minutes.
3. For each phrase:
   - **Learn it** — listen (tap 🔊, or 🐢 for slow), read the meaning and the
     pronunciation hint.
   - **Copy it** — say it out loud together.
   - **Your turn** — you see the English, you say the Spanish, and the app
     listens and tells you how you did.

Do it most nights and your **🔥 streak** climbs. Phrases you struggle with come
back over the following days (spaced repetition) so they really stick.

You can also tap any individual lesson from the **Course** list, or **Review
only** to drill what's due.

## 📚 What it teaches

Four levels, 27 lessons, ~350 phrases — all in **Castilian (Spain) Spanish**:

- **A · Holiday Survival** — greetings, numbers, café & restaurant, directions,
  shopping, hotel, emergencies & pharmacy.
- **B · Getting By** — small talk with neighbours, family, time & dates, money,
  transport, and *buying/renting your place in Spain*.
- **C · Foundation GCSE** — the present tense, daily routine, hobbies, food &
  health, weather, opinions and describing your area.
- **D · Higher GCSE** — the past (preterite & imperfect), future, conditional
  ("I would like…"), work & plans, and the environment.

Pronunciation hints use the Castilian sounds (e.g. *gracias* = "GRAH-thyahs"),
since that's what you'll hear in Mallorca and mainland Spain.

## 🔒 Privacy

Everything stays on your phone. There are no accounts, no sign-in and no servers
collecting anything — your progress and streaks are stored in the browser on
each device. Speech is processed by your phone/Safari's own built-in speech
features.

---

## ✍️ Adding or changing lessons

All the content lives in one easy-to-read file: **`js/curriculum.js`**.
Each phrase is just:

```js
{ es: "Gracias", en: "Thank you", hint: "GRAH-thyahs", note: "optional tip" }
```

To add a lesson, copy an existing `{ id, title, goal, items: [...] }` block
inside a level's `lessons` array and fill in your phrases. Optional `tip` and
`grammar` fields show on the lesson's intro screen. After editing, bump the
`CACHE` version string in `sw.js` so phones pick up the new content.
